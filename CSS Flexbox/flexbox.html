lion

Lions are large, social, carnivorous cats ({Panthera leo}) known as "king of the beasts," living in groups called prides primarily in African savannas, with a small population in India's Gir Forest. They are highly sexually dimorphic: males have prominent manes and weigh 170–230 kg, while smaller, hunting-active lionesses weigh 120–180 kg.

tiger

Tigers (Panthera tigris) are the world’s largest wild cats, renowned for their power, strength, and unique black-striped orange coats. As endangered apex predators, they are primarily found in Asian forests and mangroves, with only about 4,500 left in the wild. They are solitary hunters, excellent swimmers, and possess distinct, individual stripe patterns

chetah

The cheetah (Acinonyx jubatus) is the world's fastest land mammal, capable of reaching speeds of 60–70 mph (112 km/h) in short bursts. Known for their tawny coats with black spots and distinct "tear-stripe" facial markings, they are specialized predators inhabiting Africa and Iran. Cheetahs are vulnerable due to habitat loss.
